Steps to Make Any-night-of-the-week Traditional Bread and Celery Stuffing Tasty

To get started with this recipe, we must first prepare a few components. You can have Traditional Bread and Celery Stuffing using 8 ingredients and 7 steps. Here is how you can achieve it.

The ingredients needed to make Traditional Bread and Celery Stuffing:

  • Get 3 cups turkey broth, or as needed
  • Prepare ¼ cup butter
  • Take 1 medium onion, chopped
  • Get 1 loaf white bread
  • Prepare 1 (10 ounce) package saltine crackers
  • Make ready 1 (6 ounce) box turkey stuffing mix
  • Make ready 3 stalks celery, chopped
  • Get nonstick cooking spray

Steps to make Traditional Bread and Celery Stuffing:

  1. Empty 2 cups of broth and butter into a medium pan over medium heat. Add onions and cook for about 20 minutes.
  2. While onions cook, tear the bread into a very large mixing bowl. You don't want large pieces of crust or heel. This part of the bread doesn't absorb as well. The regular bread parts can be torn into large pieces, about 4 pieces per slice. Don't mush the bread, tear easily. Squeeze and crush the crackers in the sleeve and empty into the mixing bowl. Empty all contents of boxed stuffing into the bowl.
  3. After the onion has cooked, add the celery. Cook for another 10 to 15 minutes, turn heat off, and let cool, 10 to 15 minutes.
  4. While your mixture is cooking, preheat the oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C) and spray a roasting pan with nonstick spray.
  5. Empty one ladleful of stock at a time over the bread. After each, use a fork to softly fold/fluff the bread mixture. Don't stir or mash. Bread tearing while folding/fluffing is okay, but you don't want it mashed up or doughy. Then, add more broth to the mixture, fold/fluff, and wait about 30 seconds for absorption between adding more. The stuffing should hold together and be moist.
  6. Empty mixture into the sprayed roasting pan. Using your fork, softly fluff over the mixture until it covers the pan. Add little amounts of broth at a time and use the fork to pick up/fluff until you just start to see it not absorbing. Cover the pan with aluminum foil.
  7. Bake in the preheated oven for 45 minutes. Uncover and continue to bake until lightly browned, about 5 minutes more.
